Social Pet App

UX Concept | 2017

Intro

Social Pet marked the beginning of my UX journey, conceived during a UX course in Buenos Aires, Argentina. It's an MVP concept for a mobile app tailored for pet enthusiasts, envisioned to foster a community where users can connect over shared interests regarding pets.

Throughout this project, I navigated various stages of the UX process, from discovery to user testing, employing a range of methods:

  • Problem Definition
  • Personas
  • Benchmarking
  • Wireframing
  • Testing
  • Prototyping

The Challenge

Engaging with potential users unveiled diverse needs such as more frequent dog walks, sharing pet photos, meeting other pet owners, and exchanging experiences. A common thread among these needs was the desire for socialization.

Pet owners yearn for connections within their community as it motivates them to dedicate more time for activities with their pets, alleviating stress, separation anxiety, and behavioral issues in the animals.

I crafted various personas from user interviews and performed benchmarking to hone in on key features for the app MVP: search functionality, calendar, media sharing (images and videos), and chat.

Sketches & Wireframes

A sketch session was conducted during the course to garner initial feedback and insights from fellow designers. This feedback propelled the creation of lo-fi wireframe prototypes for user testing, to pinpoint areas of improvement within the app.

User Interface

The user interface emerged from the wireframes and user feedback, utilizing a vibrant palette to delineate key pages and establish a hierarchy among elements. This was particularly pivotal on intricate pages like the calendar, where abundant information could potentially confound users.

Takeaways

This project was a maiden voyage into applying UX design techniques and contemplating the entire process, which was a fresh terrain for me. Despite knowing that I would approach certain aspects differently now, this endeavor holds a special place in my heart as it echoes my evolution as a designer.

Given more time, I would further user-test the prototype, focusing on refining the app based on user feedback and introducing new features aligned with user needs.
